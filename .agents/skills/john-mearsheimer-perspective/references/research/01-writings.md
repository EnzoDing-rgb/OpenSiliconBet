# John J. Mearsheimer - Major Writings and Core Theses

## Major Books

### 1. *Conventional Deterrence* (1983)
- **Core Argument**: Deterrence success depends on the potential attacker's beliefs about war outcome. Deterrence fails when attacker believes it can execute a successful blitzkrieg strategy with low costs and rapid victory. Attrition and limited-aims strategies rarely lead to deterrence failure.
- **Key Prediction**: Soviet Union would not quickly defeat NATO in Central Europe because blitzkrieg was not feasible.
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Mearsheimer
- **Reliability**: Secondary

### 2. *Liddell Hart and the Weight of History* (1988)
- **Core Argument**: Reassesses B.H. Liddell Hart's intellectual legacy, arguing he distorted the historical record of blitzkrieg's origins to serve his own theoretical purposes.
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Mearsheimer
- **Reliability**: Secondary

### 3. *The Tragedy of Great Power Politics* (2001)
- **Magnum opus of offensive realism**
- **Core Argument**: 
  - Anarchic international system creates strong incentives for great powers to seek hegemony through expansion
  - States are not satisfied with existing power distributions - they constantly seek opportunities to gain power at others' expense
  - Global hegemony is impossible because of oceanic stopping power; only regional hegemony is achievable
  - Regional hegemons act as offshore balancers to prevent other regional hegemons from emerging
  - Predicts China's rise will likely lead to conflict with United States
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Tragedy_of_Great_Power_Politics
- **Reliability**: Primary (written by Mearsheimer)

### 4. *The Israel Lobby and U.S. Foreign Policy* (2007, with Stephen Walt)
- **Core Argument**: A loose coalition of individuals and organizations (the "Israel lobby") wields disproportionate influence over U.S. foreign policy in the Middle East, pushing policies that are not in the U.S. national interest.
- **Key Points**: Not a Jewish lobby - includes Christian Zionists; not a conspiracy, just a powerful interest group like the NRA. Argues U.S.-Israel alliance skews policy toward Israel, harming regional stability.
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Israel_Lobby_and_U.S._Foreign_Policy
- **Reliability**: Primary (co-authored by Mearsheimer)

### 5. *Why Leaders Lie: The Truth About Lying in International Politics* (2011)
- **Core Argument**: Leaders lie to their publics about international affairs more often than commonly thought, but lies between states are rare. Different types of international lies serve specific strategic purposes.
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Why_Leaders_Lie:_The_Truth_About_Lying_in_International_Politics
- **Reliability**: Primary (written by Mearsheimer)

### 6. *The Great Delusion: Liberal Dreams and International Realities* (2018)
- **Core Argument**: The liberal international order promoted by the U.S. after the Cold War is a delusion that cannot be sustained globally. Liberal hegemony leads to unnecessary conflict and ultimately undermines itself.
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Great_Delusion:_Liberal_Dreams_and_International_Realities
- **Reliability**: Primary (written by Mearsheimer)

### 7. *How States Think: The Rationality of Foreign Policy* (2023, with Sebastian Rosato)
- **Core Argument**: Defends rational choice theory in international relations, argues that states consistently act rationally based on their calculation of power and interests.
- **Source**: https://www.mearsheimer.com/publications/
- **Reliability**: Primary (co-authored by Mearsheimer)

## Core Theses Repeated Across Works

1. **Offensive Realism**: The anchor of all Mearsheimer's work. Anarchic system compels great powers to maximize power to survive, leading to constant security competition.

2. **Skepticism of International Institutions**: Institutions cannot fundamentally change state behavior or mitigate the security competition that comes from anarchy. ("The False Promise of International Institutions", 1994)

3. **Criticism of Democratic Peace Theory**: Mearsheimer rejects the claim that democracies don't fight each other, arguing the evidence is weak and theoretical foundations are flawed.

4. **Offshore Balancing**: U.S. should act as an offshore balancer, not pursue global hegemony or nation-building. This prescription appears in most of his policy work.

5. **Power Determines Outcomes**: Structural power distribution, not ideology or institutions, is the primary determinant of international outcomes.

## Self-Created Terms and Concepts

1. **Offensive Realism** - Structural realist theory emphasizing that states seek hegemony
2. **Stopping Power of Water** - Argument that oceans prevent global hegemony by making cross-oceanic conquest and control impossible
3. **Regional Hegemony** - The only achievable level of hegemony; one dominant power in a geographic region
4. **Blitzkrieg Strategy** - Classification of attack strategy that leads to deterrence failure
5. **Attrition Strategy** - High-cost, high-uncertainty attack strategy that rarely leads to deterrence failure
6. **Limited Aims Strategy** - Low-gain, high-risk middle ground between blitzkrieg and attrition
7. **Rollback** - Popuplarized term for geopolitical strategy of inciting unrest and promoting regime change in rival states
