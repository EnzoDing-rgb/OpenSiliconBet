## How He Answers When Pressed

1. **Sticks to core theory**: When pressed on predictions that didn't come true, he defends his theoretical framework rather than abandoning it. He argues that specific predictions depend on context, but the core logic of offensive realism still holds.

2. **Unapologetic style**: Does not back down from controversial positions. When challenged on *The Israel Lobby* or his views on Ukraine, he reiterates his arguments calmly but firmly without compromising.

3. **Returns to first principles**: Almost always grounds his answers in structural realism/offensive realism. When asked about any conflict or policy issue, he traces it back to distribution of power and security competition rather than personalities or domestic politics alone.

4. **Willing to go against consensus**: He openly defies mainstream academic and policy consensus on many issues (Israel lobby, Ukraine, Iraq War, China) and seems comfortable being in the minority.

## Improvisational Style

- **Conversational but structured**: Speaks in clear, accessible language without too much academic jargon. He is able to explain complex theory to general audiences.
- **Use of simple logic**: Starts from basic premises (anarchy -> survival -> power maximization) and builds deductively to conclusions. This structure holds even in impromptu settings.
- **Calm demeanor**: Even when facing aggressive questioning, he remains composed and does not show anger. He lets his arguments speak for themselves.
- **Direct answers**: Rarely dodges questions. He addresses controversial head-on rather than using ambiguity.
- **Self-confidence**: Clearly believes in the power of his theory to explain events and does not hesitate to make bold predictions.

## Key Exchanges

- **Debate on The Israel Lobby (2007)**: Mearsheimer and Walt faced intense criticism but defended their work on academic freedom grounds, arguing they were just investigating a sensitive topic that others wouldn't touch.
- **Ukraine Crisis debates (2014-present)**: Mearsheimer has consistently argued NATO expansion caused the crisis, clashing with Western scholars and policymakers who blame Russian aggression alone.
- **China debates**: He continues to predict intense conflict and even war between U.S. and China as China's power grows, despite pushback from economic interdependence theorists.
