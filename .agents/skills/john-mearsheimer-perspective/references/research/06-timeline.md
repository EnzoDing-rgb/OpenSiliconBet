# John J. Mearsheimer - Timeline

## 1947-1970: Early Life and Education
- **December 14, 1947**: Born in Brooklyn, New York City, to Thomas Joseph Mearsheimer (civil engineer and Air Force colonel) and Ruth Margaret Baumann. Of German and Irish descent.
- **1955 (age 8)**: Family moves to Croton-on-Hudson, New York.
- **1964 (age 17)**: Enlists in United States Army.
- **1966-1970**: Attends United States Military Academy at West Point.
- **1970**: Graduates from West Point with BS degree. Marries Mary T. Cobb. Commissioned as second lieutenant in US Air Force.

## 1970-1981: Graduate Education and Early Career
- **1970-1975**: Serves in US Air Force, rising to captain.
- **1974**: Earns MA in international relations from University of Southern California while still in Air Force.
- **1975**: Resigns from Air Force to enter Cornell University for graduate studies.
- **1978**: Earns MA in government from Cornell.
- **1978-1979**: Peace studies fellowship at Cornell.
- **1979-1980**: Research fellow at Brookings Institution in Washington DC.
- **1980**: Earns PhD in government from Cornell (completed 1981).

## 1980-1990: Early Academic Career
- **1980-1982**: Postdoctoral research associate at Harvard University's Center for International Affairs.
- **1982**: Joins University of Chicago political science faculty as assistant professor. Begins long career at Chicago that continues to present.
- **1983**: Publishes first book *Conventional Deterrence*. Wins Edgar S. Furniss Jr. Book Award.
- **1984**: Promoted to associate professor at Chicago. Wins Quantrell Award for Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching.
- **1987**: Promoted to full professor.
- **1988**: Publishes *Liddell Hart and the Weight of History*.
- **1989-1992**: Serves as chair of Department of Political Science at University of Chicago.

## 1990s: Theoretical Development and Controversy
- **1994**: Publishes "The False Promise of International Institutions" in *International Security* - sparks major debate with neoliberal institutionalists.
- **1996**: Appointed R. Wendell Harrison Distinguished Service Professor of Political Science at University of Chicago.

## 2000s: Major Works and Controversy
- **2001**: Publishes magnum opus *The Tragedy of Great Power Politics* which fully lays out offensive realism. Wins Lepgold Book Prize.
- **2003**: Elected to American Academy of Arts and Sciences. Opposes 2003 Iraq War publicly before invasion.
- **2006**: Publishes with Stephen Walt working paper "The Israel Lobby and U.S. Foreign Policy" in *London Review of Books* - generates enormous controversy.
- **2007**: Publishes expanded book version *The Israel Lobby and U.S. Foreign Policy* becomes New York Times bestseller.

## 2010s: Later Career and New Directions
- **2010**: Gives "The Future of Palestine" lecture, argues two-state solution is dead and Israel will become an apartheid state. More controversy.
- **2011**: Publishes *Why Leaders Lie: The Truth About Lying in International Politics*.
- **2014**: Publishes "Why the Ukraine Crisis is the West's Fault" in *Foreign Affairs* - generates new controversy.
- **2018**: Publishes *The Great Delusion: Liberal Dreams and International Realities* criticizing the liberal international order. Debates LIO with Anne-Marie Slaughter and John Ikenberry.

## 2020s: Present Activity
- **2020**: Receives American Political Science Association's James Madison Award - highest honor for American political scientists.
- **2022-2023**: Continues to speak and write extensively on Russia-Ukraine war, maintaining his position that NATO expansion caused the conflict.
- **2023**: Publishes *How States Think: The Rationality of Foreign Policy with Sebastian Rosato (Yale University Press). Continues active teaching and public speaking.
- **2025-2026**: Remains active as emeritus/professor at University of Chicago, continues publishing and public commentary.

## Key Turning Points in Thinking

1. **Cornell Graduate Training**: Developed realist perspective under guidance of realist scholars, moved away from any liberal views he held earlier.
2. **1980s conventional deterrence work**: Started thinking about how military strategy affects deterrence outcomes, laid groundwork for later offensive realism.
3. **Break from defensive realism (1990s**: Decisive break with Kenneth Waltz's defensive realism to develop own offensive realism theory - his signature contribution.
4. **Post-Cold War**: Rejected the liberal consensus that history had ended with liberal victory, stuck with realist perspective.
5. **Moving to policy relevance**: Consciously chose to engage public debates from 2000s onward, becoming one of the most publicly visible IR scholars in the world.

## Current Status (2026)
- **Age**: 78
- **Position**: R. Wendell Harrison Distinguished Service Professor Emeritus/Professor at University of Chicago
- **Activity**: Still active in public debate, continues writing, publishing, and giving interviews.
