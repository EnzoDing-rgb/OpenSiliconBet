# Robert Jervis - Timeline (1940-2021)

## Early Life and Education

**April 30, 1940**: Born in New York City
- Grew up in politically active liberal New York circles in the 1940s-1950s
- Developed early interest in current events and Cold War politics

**1950s**: As a student, organized current events newsletter in 5th grade
- Handed out leaflets for Adlai Stevenson in 1952 and 1956 presidential campaigns

**1962**: Graduated from Oberlin College with BA
- Developed interest in nuclear strategy
- Discovered Thomas Schelling's *Strategy of Conflict* and Glenn Snyder's *Deterrence and Defense* which shaped his intellectual trajectory

**1962**: Began graduate work at University of California, Berkeley
- Studied under Glenn Snyder
- Participated in the Free Speech Movement
- Opposed the Vietnam War and came to believe the U.S. could not win

**1968**: Received PhD from UC Berkeley
- Dissertation work on signaling and images in international relations

## Early Career

**1968-1972**: Assistant Professor of Government at Harvard University
- Invited by Thomas Schelling to join the Center for International Affairs
- Developed close friendships with Thomas Schelling and Kenneth Waltz

**1970**: Published first book *The Logic of Images in International Relations* (Princeton University Press)
- First major work on signaling and perception

**1972**: Promoted to Associate Professor at Harvard

**1974**: Candidate for the tenured chair vacated by Henry Kissinger at Harvard
- The department did not approve his tenure
- Left Harvard for UCLA

**1974-1980**: Professor of Political Science at University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA)

**1976**: Published *Perception and Misperception in International Politics* (Princeton University Press)
- His most famous work - established him as the leading scholar in political psychology and IR

**1978**: Published "Cooperation under the Security Dilemma" in *World Politics*
- Most influential journal article - refined the concept of the security dilemma and introduced the offense-defense balance framework
- Still widely cited and taught

**1978**: Began consulting for the CIA
- Would continue this relationship for over 30 years
- Served as head of the CIA Historical Review Panel for 20 years

## Columbia University Years

**1980**: Joined Columbia University faculty as Adlai E. Stevenson Professor of International Politics
- Remained at Columbia until his death in 2021
- Affiliated with the Arnold A. Saltzman Institute of War and Peace Studies

**1985**: Published *The Illogic of American Nuclear Strategy* (Cornell University Press)
- First major statement of his nuclear revolution argument

**1989**: Published *The Meaning of the Nuclear Revolution* (Cornell University Press)
- Won the 1990 Grawemeyer Award for Ideas Improving World Order

**1997**: Published *System Effects: Complexity in Political and Social Life* (Princeton University Press)
- Jervis considered this his most important book
- Developed theory of complex interconnected systems and unintended consequences

**2000-2001**: Served as President of the American Political Science Association

**2005**: Published *American Foreign Policy in a New Era* (Routledge)

**2006**: Awarded NAS Award for Behavior Research Relevant to the Prevention of Nuclear War from the National Academy of Sciences

**2010**: Published *Why Intelligence Fails: Lessons From The Iranian Revolution And The Iraq War* (Cornell University Press)
- Based on his experiences with CIA - analyzed two major intelligence failures

**2010**: Participated in the Hertog Global Strategy Initiative on nuclear proliferation

**2017**: Published *How Statesmen Think: The Psychology of International Politics* (Princeton University Press)
- Collected essays on political psychology, late career reflection

**2020**: Published "Robert Jervis on Learning the Scholar's Craft" - H-Diplo reflective autobiographical essay

**April 2021**: Elected to the United States National Academy of Sciences

## Death and Legacy

**December 9, 2021**: Died of lung cancer in his Manhattan home at age 81
- Columbia University established the annual Robert Jervis Conference in his honor
- Posthumous volume *The Jervis Effect: The Scholarship and Legacy of Robert Jervis* to be published 2025 by Columbia University Press

## Key Turning Points in His Thinking

1. **Graduate school at Berkeley**: Shifted from current events to theoretical analysis - learned from Schelling and Snyder about strategic interaction

2. **Free Speech Movement and Vietnam**: Learned firsthand about political complexity, the limits of prediction, and how even participants can misunderstand the whole situation

3. **CIA consulting**: Gave him inside access to policy debates that shaped his views on nuclear strategy and intelligence failures - confirmed his skepticism about official hawkish arguments

4. **Invitation to contribute to Schelling festschrift**: Led him to develop his ideas about system effects, which became his longest-running theoretical interest
